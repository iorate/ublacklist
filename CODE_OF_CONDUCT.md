# Code of Conduct

This document defines the participation rules and enforcement policy for the uBlacklist project. It applies to all interactions across the project's repositories, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and commit comments, by any participant.

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Spam, excessive bulk activity, and sustained disruption of the project.
- Contributions that ignore maintainer feedback, and others that waste maintainer time.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in the context of this project.

## Enforcement

The maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and may ban any participant from the project's repositories for sustained or serious violations.

All bans are permanent. Maintainers may take any such action at their sole discretion and without prior warning. Decisions are final within the project.

Concerns about a maintainer's own conduct may be reported to GitHub Support under their Acceptable Use Policies.
